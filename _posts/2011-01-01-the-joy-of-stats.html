I just watched an hour long documentary on statistics.

Here's what I learned:

The average person spends 24 years of his or her life asleep.

The study of statistics began two centuries ago as a way for the state to monitor the people. The word "statistics" actually comes from the word "state." Now, through organizations such as Crimespotting, citizens are using statistics to keep the government accountable.

In San Francisco, crime clusters around the flat areas and is sparse on steep hills (I'm suddenly not so bitter about the 45 degree hill I must climb every time I walk home).

There is so. much. information. in the world. The question is: how will we use it?

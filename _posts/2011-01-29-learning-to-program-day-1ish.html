I've been meaning to learn to program for a while now. I've worked for two companies that use Ruby, so I chose to start with Ruby. I went to a weekend-long Rails outreach for women workshop. As a total beginner, it was highly confusing. I told myself I would go home and continue to learn, but I got distracted and here I am 3 months later and not much more program-y.

This week I've been trying to change that. I downloaded a PDF of Chris Pine's Learn to Program onto my Kindle and I'm reading it. (side note: reading PDFs on a Kindle is the worst. Is there a way to make PDFs into Kindle format?) Today, I started a page on my personal wiki to store the Ruby resources I've found over the past few months but never bothered to categorize. Then, I looked up more resources and searched Quora for good ways to start learning Ruby.

I even watched this one-hour MIT lecture from their undergrad intro to computer programming course. It's about Python, but given my total lack of knowledge on programming, learning the basics in any way I can is a big help. Plus, I love the way this professor uses the word "administrivia." Plus, sitting down in front of a lecture (as opposed to doing a tutorial) brings me back to college and my Pavlovian response is to actually pay attention and take notes as opposed to going in the kitchen to make myself yet another PB&J.

Here I go.
